## The Abstract

Large Language Models systematically disadvantage non-English languages at every level. A Kannada sentence requires **3–5× more tokens** than its English equivalent, costs proportionally more to process, and produces lower-quality outputs. This research traces the cascade from byte-level tokenization through attention mechanisms to final generation.

## Key Research Findings

### 1. The Fertility Crisis
Token fertility is the average number of tokens per word. My research quantified that equivalent semantic content for Indic languages costs up to 15x more in positional encoding slots.

| Language | GPT-4 Fertility vs English | GPT-4o Reduction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kannada** | ~3–4× | 79% Improvement |
| **Malayalam**| ~4–5× | 79% Improvement |
| **Hindi** | ~2–3× | 64% Improvement |

### 2. The Economic Inequity
The "Script Tax" means non-English speakers obtain worse results for higher costs. At scale, a Hindi-based service pays roughly **$300,000 more annually** than an identical English service due to token inefficiency.

### 3. The Context Window Shrinkage
A 128K-token context window effectively shrinks to **25,000–42,000 tokens** of usable content for Hindi or Arabic, severely limiting RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) applications for world languages.

## Technical Analysis: Byte-Level Tokenization

I analyzed how **Byte Pair Encoding (BPE)** creates a fundamental tax. Since UTF-8 characters for Kannada consume 3 bytes while English consumes 1, the BPE algorithm needs more merges to constitute a single character. 

Because English dominates training data (95% in Llama 3), English byte-pair merges are learned first. Non-Latin byte sequences often never get merged into complete characters, leaving individual bytes as tokens—destroying semantic integrity.

## The "Gemini Moment"

This research was sparked by a real-world test: Asking Gemini to write a Kannada anchoring speech for a cultural festival (Sankranti). Despite Kannada representing only **0.019% of common crawl data**, the model produced culturally nuanced prose, leading me to investigate the cross-lingual embedding spaces that enable this high performance against massive data odds.

---

## Solutions Investigated

- **MAGNET (NeurIPS 2024)**: Adaptive tokenization.
- **Vocabulary Expansion**: Parameter freezing with subword initialization.
- **Sovereign Models**: The rise of IndicTrans2 and Sarvam-1.
